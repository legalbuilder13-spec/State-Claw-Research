# R15.CompletenessGate

**Spec:** Every deliverable must pass a 7-criterion completeness check before delivery: (1) Definitions retrieved for every chapter under analysis; (2) Operative provisions retrieved for every claim; (3) Statutory exemptions traced (if the analysis turns on whether an exemption applies); (4) Implementing regulations retrieved for every delegation clause flagged by R7; (5) Cross-references resolved per R8 (each cross-ref retrieved or explicitly skipped with reason); (6) Currency confirmed per R10 (no stale sources beyond threshold without explicit annotation); (7) Classification overlay applied per R12 (every category flagged or cleared). A failure on any criterion blocks delivery; the deliverable is replaced with a "research incomplete" notice listing the failing criteria.
**Enforcement point:** Orchestrator runs the completeness check in `before_agent_finalize` after the Drafter, Verifier, Critic, Confidence, and Classification sub-agents have all completed. The check produces a `completeness-output` artifact (per `schemas/completeness-output.schema.json`) with a pass/fail per criterion.
**Severity:** blocking.
**Failure mode:** Renderer not invoked; orchestrator emits a structured "research incomplete" notice (template at `company-context/deliverable-templates/research-incomplete.md`) listing exactly which of the 7 criteria failed and what would be required to resolve each. The notice is delivered via the same channel as a normal deliverable.

## Detail

R15 is the final gate. It's the rule that says: even if every other rule passed at its individual hook, the aggregate research must satisfy a completeness contract before the deliverable goes out. The 7 criteria are not arbitrary — they correspond directly to PRD §6 Methodology Part II's 7-step research sequence per state. R15 is the procedural enforcement of that sequence.

**The 7 criteria.**

1. **Definitions.** For every statutory chapter under analysis (per R9), the chapter's Definitions section was retrieved (or marked `definitions_inline` per corpus metadata).
2. **Operative provisions.** Every claim in the deliverable's body has a cite to an operative provision in `retrieval_log` (per R3).
3. **Exemptions.** When the analysis turns on whether a statutory exemption applies (detected via the analysis's `relies_on_exemption: true` flag set by the Drafter), the relevant exemption provisions were retrieved and analyzed. This catches the failure mode where a deliverable says "the worker is an employee" without checking whether any of the statutory exemptions might apply.
4. **Regulations.** Every delegation clause flagged by R7 has a resolved implementing regulation (or an explicit `delegation_unresolved` annotation with a documented reason).
5. **Cross-references.** Every cross-reference flagged by R8 is resolved (retrieved or skipped with reason). No silent omissions.
6. **Currency.** Per R10, every cited source's `current_through` is either within threshold OR explicitly annotated as stale. No silent staleness.
7. **Classification overlay.** Per R12, every category in `risk-taxonomy.yaml` is flagged or cleared with rationale.

**Why these 7 specifically.** Each criterion catches a distinct failure mode that has been observed in production legal AI:

| Criterion | Failure mode it prevents |
|---|---|
| Definitions | Applying everyday meaning to a defined term |
| Operative | Producing a claim with no retrieval backing |
| Exemptions | Concluding "the rule applies" without checking carve-outs |
| Regulations | Stopping at the statute when the operative content is in the implementing reg |
| Cross-references | Skipping operative content in a referenced subdivision |
| Currency | Producing answers based on superseded law |
| Classification | Generic legal answer with no operator-specific risk mapping |

**The Drafter signals "relies_on_exemption" explicitly.** Criterion 3 needs to know whether the analysis turns on an exemption. The Drafter signals this with a boolean field on the claim; if true, R15 requires the exemption provisions to be in `retrieval_log`. The Drafter sets this true when the analysis form is "the rule says X, but exemption Y applies, so the result is Z." Drafters that set this false but actually rely on an exemption are caught by the Critic (separate enforcement, not R15).

**Completeness vs. correctness.** R15 checks that the research is *complete* (all 7 procedural criteria satisfied). It does not check whether the *conclusion* is correct — that's beyond any rule's scope. A deliverable can pass R15 with all 7 criteria green and still reach a wrong conclusion if the legal interpretation is wrong; that's why every deliverable is stamped "DRAFT FOR COUNSEL REVIEW."

**R11 vs. R15 distinction.** R15 is mechanistic (each criterion is yes/no); R11 is judgment-driven (confidence rating over a richer criteria set including Verifier results and revise-loop counts). R15 fires before R11. If R15 fails, R11 doesn't even run. If R15 passes, R11 evaluates whether the *quality* of the satisfied criteria is HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW. Both are gates; they catch different categories of failure.

**Audit chain.** R15's pass/fail per criterion is logged to the audit chain along with the timestamps and the retrieved doc_ids that satisfied each criterion. This produces a one-row-per-deliverable summary that auditors can inspect: "this deliverable on 2026-05-15 satisfied all 7 criteria with the following retrievals: ..."

## Interactions with other rules

- **R3, R7, R8, R9, R10, R12** each map to one criterion in R15's table. R15 is the rollup; the individual rules are the per-criterion enforcement.
- **R11 LowConfidenceGate** runs after R15. Both can block; both produce structured "incomplete" responses (with different templates).
- **R13 VerifyFlagCap** is orthogonal — R13 can fire even when R15 passes, if the deliverable is complete but has too many open verify questions.

## Test cases

✅ Compliant: all 7 criteria pass
```
Run on California AB5 question:
1. Definitions: ca-lab-2750-2773 retrieved. ✓
2. Operative: ca-lab-2775 retrieved; all claims cite it. ✓
3. Exemptions: relies_on_exemption: false; criterion not applicable. ✓
4. Regulations: § 2775 has no delegation; criterion not applicable. ✓
5. Cross-refs: 2 cross-refs in § 2775, both retrieved. ✓
6. Currency: all sources within 90-day threshold. ✓
7. Classification overlay: 8/8 categories addressed. ✓
Aggregate: all green. Deliverable proceeds to Renderer.
```

❌ Non-compliant: missing exemption analysis
```
Drafter concludes: "the worker is an employee under § 2775(a)" without checking the 7-prong exemption in § 2775(b).
The Drafter set relies_on_exemption: false, but the Critic flagged it as inconsistent (the conclusion form implies an exemption analysis is required).
R15 criterion 3: fails. Aggregate: incomplete.
Result: "research incomplete" notice listing the missing exemption analysis. Recommended next step: retrieve § 2775(b) and re-run.
```

❌ Non-compliant: unresolved cross-references
```
Run includes ca-lab-2775 which references ca-lab-2776 and the IWC wage orders.
2776 was retrieved; the wage orders were not (Drafter skipped with no reason recorded).
R15 criterion 5: fails. Aggregate: incomplete.
Result: notice listing the missing cross-ref. Drafter could either add a skip reason or retrieve the wage orders.
```

❌ Non-compliant: classification overlay missing category
```
risk-taxonomy.yaml has 8 categories; classification_overlay block has 7.
R15 criterion 7: fails.
Result: notice listing the missing category. Classification sub-agent invoked to complete.
```

❌ Non-compliant: stale corpus, no annotation, no refresh
```
Cited statute current_through 180 days ago; threshold is 90 days; TaskSpec.requires_fresh: false.
R10 marked as warning (not blocking), so the annotation should appear on the cite.
But the Drafter's output didn't include the annotation.
R15 criterion 6: fails because the staleness is not explicitly annotated in the deliverable.
Result: notice; Drafter adds annotation; re-render.
```

⚠️ Edge case: criterion not applicable
```
A research question with no delegations and no exemptions:
Criterion 3 (exemptions): N/A. Marked as such; doesn't count as failure.
Criterion 4 (regulations): N/A.
Aggregate: green for the applicable criteria.
```

## Open questions

- **Adding criteria.** The 7-criterion list reflects v1 scope. Phase 4+ may surface additional structural failure modes that warrant new criteria (e.g., "burden-of-proof allocation explicitly addressed when the question turns on who must prove what"). Adding a criterion requires updating this file + `completeness-output.schema.json` + the orchestrator's check logic.
- **Per-jurisdiction criteria.** Some criteria are more or less important per jurisdiction (e.g., implementing regulations are critical in CA but less so in IL where the statutes are typically more detailed). The current 7 criteria are uniform across jurisdictions; per-jurisdiction tuning is a Phase 4 refinement.
- **N/A vs. fail.** A criterion marked N/A doesn't fail aggregate, but a criterion marked N/A *incorrectly* (e.g., the Drafter said "no delegations" but R7 detected one) is a Critic-level bug. The Critic separately checks N/A claims against the underlying signal sources.
- **Auto-resolution attempts.** Some criterion failures could be auto-resolved (e.g., cross-ref retrievals can fire automatically). Current design: orchestrator attempts auto-resolution up to 2 iterations; persistent failure surfaces as "research incomplete." Iteration cap configurable in `r15-config.json`.
