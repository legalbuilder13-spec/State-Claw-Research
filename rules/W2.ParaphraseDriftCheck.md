# W2.ParaphraseDriftCheck

**Spec:** Between two Drafter revisions of the same claim (e.g., across a `before_agent_finalize` revise loop), if a paraphrase has drifted from its anchored source — meaning the lexical-similarity score between the paraphrase and the source span has dropped below a configurable threshold (default: cosine similarity of 0.65 on `voyage-3-large` embeddings, OR a stricter check using BLEU-4 score < 0.4 against the source span) — the Critic emits a structured warning naming the claim, the source span, the previous paraphrase, and the current paraphrase.
**Enforcement point:** The Critic sub-agent runs after every Drafter revision. It computes paraphrase-drift scores for each claim that has a paraphrased (non-quoted) assertion with a cite to a retrieved source. The warning is added to the deliverable's `warnings[]` block.
**Severity:** warning.
**Failure mode:** Deliverable is annotated with the warning; not blocked. The reviewer sees a side-by-side comparison in the audit trail: "Revision 1 paraphrase: X. Revision 2 paraphrase: Y. Source span: Z." The reviewer judges whether the drift is acceptable. If drift accumulates across multiple revisions or affects a high-stakes claim, R11 may fire from the same Critic pass.

## Detail

W2 catches a subtle pattern that R3 and R4 don't cover: the Drafter writes a paraphrase, the orchestrator triggers a revise loop for some other reason (e.g., a cite needs to be added), the Drafter rewrites the paraphrase incidentally during the revision, and the rewrite drifts further from the source. R3 says the cite must exist; R4 says quotes need anchors; but a paraphrase that's been re-written under revise can quietly drift across revisions without violating either.

Concrete example: the source span reads "the worker is free from the hiring entity's control and direction." Revision 1's paraphrase says "the worker performs independently of the hiring entity's direction." Revision 2's paraphrase says "the worker has discretion in how to perform the work." Revision 2 has lost the load-bearing word "free" and quietly inserted "discretion" — different legal concept. Each revision was driven by some adjacent fix (a cite added, a different sentence restructured), but the paraphrase quietly drifted under the cover of the revise loop.

**Two similarity metrics.** W2 uses two complementary checks:

1. **Embedding similarity.** Compute `voyage-3-large` embeddings of both the paraphrase and the source span; compute cosine similarity. Catches semantic drift (different concepts) but may miss subtle lexical drift (right concept, wrong qualifier).
2. **BLEU-4 against source.** Compute BLEU score of paraphrase against source. Catches lexical drift (drift in load-bearing words like "shall" vs. "may"). Embedding similarity stays high here because the surface form is similar.

Either metric below threshold triggers the warning. Both are configurable in `extensions/agentic-law-os/w2-config.json`.

**Cross-revision comparison.** W2 specifically compares revision N to revision N-1, not just revision N to source. This is because a paraphrase might be acceptable at revision 1, get "improved" in revision 2 in a way that introduces drift, and the drift only shows up by comparing the two. Comparing revision N to source alone catches some drift but misses revision-induced drift.

**Why this is a warning, not blocking.** Paraphrasing is necessary — quoting everything is impractical and unreadable. Some drift is inevitable in legitimate paraphrasing (rephrasing for clarity, removing legalese). A reviewer can typically judge "this drift is fine" or "this drift changes the legal meaning" faster than the agent can. W2 surfaces the drift; the reviewer makes the call.

**Threshold tuning.** The 0.65 cosine and 0.4 BLEU thresholds are starting points. After ~50 deliverables in production, the team should review W2-flagged paraphrases and assess: were the flags meaningful or noisy? Tighten thresholds if too noisy, loosen if missing real drift.

**Interaction with W3.** W3 HedgeDensity may also fire on a paraphrase that hedges away the source's strength. If both fire on the same claim, both annotations appear in the deliverable. They're not redundant — they detect different patterns.

## Interactions with other rules

- **R3 NoMemoryStatutes** requires every paraphrase to cite a retrieved source; W2 checks whether the paraphrase has drifted from that source across revisions.
- **R4 QuotingRule** governs *quoted* spans (must have hash anchors); W2 governs *paraphrased* spans (no anchor required, but drift is annotated).
- **R5 HashEcho** is the per-quote drift check; W2 is the per-paraphrase drift check. R5 blocks; W2 annotates.
- **R11 LowConfidenceGate** can fire alongside W2 if drift accumulates across multiple revisions or affects a load-bearing claim.

## Test cases

⚠️ Warning fires: semantic drift
```
Source span (ca-lab-2775(a)): "a person providing labor or services for remuneration shall be considered an employee"
Revision 1 paraphrase: "individuals doing paid work are treated as employees"
Revision 2 paraphrase: "people who work for pay can be classified as employees"
Drift: "shall be considered" (mandatory) → "can be classified" (permissive). Embedding cosine 0.61; BLEU-4 0.32.
W2 fires. Deliverable annotated:
"⚠️ Paraphrase drift between revisions detected for claim 'definition of employee'. Source uses 'shall be considered' (mandatory); revision 2 paraphrase uses 'can be classified' (permissive). Reviewer: verify whether the change in modality is intentional."
```

⚠️ Warning fires: lexical drift while semantic stable
```
Source: "the hiring entity must establish that the worker is free from the entity's control"
Revision 1: "the hiring entity bears the burden to show the worker has discretion"
Revision 2: "the hiring entity must demonstrate that the worker has autonomy from the entity's direction"
Embedding cosine: 0.78 (semantic similarity stable). BLEU-4: 0.34 (lexical drift).
W2 fires. Reviewer judges whether "autonomy" vs. "free from control" is acceptable rephrasing or material drift.
```

✅ No warning: stable paraphrase
```
Revision 1: "the worker is treated as an employee unless the employer satisfies the ABC test"
Revision 2: "a worker is presumed an employee unless the hiring entity satisfies the ABC test"
Embedding cosine: 0.94. BLEU-4: 0.62.
No drift; W2 does not fire.
```

⚠️ Warning fires: quiet introduction of qualifier
```
Source: "regulations adopted by the Commissioner"
Revision 1: "regulations promulgated by the Commissioner"
Revision 2: "regulations and informal guidance issued by the Commissioner"  // "informal guidance" inserted, not in source
Embedding cosine: 0.71. BLEU-4: 0.38.
W2 fires. Reviewer sees "informal guidance" was added in revision 2 and doesn't appear in the source.
```

✅ No warning: no revision (single-pass)
```
Drafter produced one revision. There is no prior revision to compare against.
W2 still runs against source-only comparison. If similarity below threshold, fires with "no prior revision; drift measured against source only."
If similarity above threshold, no warning.
```

## Open questions

- **Threshold calibration.** 0.65 cosine + 0.4 BLEU are guesses. Real-world tuning is required.
- **Embedding model choice.** `voyage-3-large` is the assumed embedding. Should W2 use a legal-domain-specific embedding (e.g., `voyage-law-2`) instead? Phase 4 benchmarking. The legal embedding might catch more meaningful drift but also have different threshold ranges.
- **Compute cost.** W2 runs an embedding call per paraphrased claim per revision. Modest cost but accumulates over multi-state surveys. If costs become a concern, gate W2 on `relies_on_paraphrase: true` claims only.
- **Single-revision drift detection.** When only one revision exists, W2 falls back to source-only comparison. Should this be a separate warning code (W2a) to make the distinction visible? Maybe — Phase 4 enhancement.
