# R11.LowConfidenceGate

**Spec:** The Confidence sub-agent rates every deliverable HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW based on a rule-based determination over the run's claim ledger, retrieval log, verifier results, and any blocking-rule violations. A LOW rating blocks delivery via `before_agent_finalize`; the agent emits a "research incomplete" notice listing the specific gap(s) that drove the LOW rating, rather than producing a degraded deliverable.
**Enforcement point:** The Confidence sub-agent (`api.runtime.subagent.run({ session: "agent:main:subagent:confidence", ... })`) runs after the Verifier and Critic complete; its `confidence-output` artifact (per `schemas/confidence-output.schema.json`) names a level and a per-criterion rationale. The orchestrator gates delivery on the level via `before_agent_finalize`.
**Severity:** blocking.
**Failure mode:** When the rating is LOW, the Renderer is not invoked; instead the orchestrator emits a structured "research incomplete" response listing every failed confidence criterion with a one-line explanation. The response is delivered via the same channel adapter as a normal deliverable (Slack message, etc.) so the requester sees a clear "I can't answer this yet — here's why" instead of silence or a fabricated answer. The incomplete-response template lives at `company-context/deliverable-templates/research-incomplete.md`.

## Detail

R11 is the rule that converts "the model is uncertain" into "we don't deliver." Most legal-AI failure modes look like: the model hedges everything, emits a sea of caveats, and delivers an answer that is technically defensible but practically unusable. R11's design assumption is the opposite: a deliverable that doesn't meet the confidence bar is *more useful* as a clean "I don't know yet, here's what's missing" than as a hedged-to-death partial answer.

**Rule-based confidence, not model-judged.** The Confidence sub-agent does NOT ask the LLM "how confident are you in this answer?" — that question reliably elicits over-confident or sycophantically-hedged responses depending on prompt phrasing. Instead, the sub-agent runs a deterministic checklist over the run's artifacts:

| Criterion | Threshold for HIGH | MEDIUM | LOW |
|---|---|---|---|
| Definitions retrieved (R9 satisfied) | Yes | Yes | No |
| Operative provisions retrieved | All claims have backing | ≥80% of claims backed | <80% |
| Cross-refs traced (R8 satisfied) | All resolved or explicitly skipped | ≥1 unresolved | ≥3 unresolved |
| Implementing regs retrieved if delegated (R7) | All resolved or none required | ≥1 unresolved with reason | Unresolved without reason |
| Currency confirmed (R10) | All sources fresh | Some stale but annotated | Stale + requires_fresh true |
| Verifier passed (R5) | All anchors pass | ≤1 minor mismatch resolved by revise | ≥2 unresolved mismatches |
| Drafter revise count | 0 or 1 | 2 | >2 |
| Verify flags emitted (R13) | 0 | 1–2 | ≥3 |
| Classification overlay complete (R12) | All categories addressed | All categories addressed | Missing categories |

The aggregate: HIGH if all criteria HIGH; MEDIUM if any criterion MEDIUM and none LOW; LOW if any criterion LOW. This is intentionally strict — one LOW criterion drags the whole rating to LOW.

**What "research incomplete" looks like.** The incomplete response is a structured notice, not a free-form apology. It names:

1. **The original question** (verbatim).
2. **The TaskSpec's jurisdictions and topic.**
3. **A bulleted list of what was retrieved** (so the reviewer knows what was attempted).
4. **A bulleted list of failed criteria with one-line explanations**: e.g., "❌ Cross-refs traced: 2 unresolved cross-refs from § 2775(b) to § 2776 and to the IWC wage orders. Retrieval of those would change the analysis."
5. **A "next step" recommendation**: typically "Re-ingest <jurisdiction> for <kind>" or "Add <jurisdiction> to TaskSpec.jurisdictions" or "Question requires human research — see the listed gaps."

The response is stamped "DRAFT FOR COUNSEL REVIEW" like any other deliverable, plus an additional banner: "RESEARCH INCOMPLETE — DO NOT RELY ON THIS DRAFT."

**Why this is better than hedging.** A hedged deliverable communicates "here's an answer, please double-check it." An incomplete-response communicates "here's what's missing, the answer can't be produced yet." The first invites the reviewer to skim and rely on the answer; the second forces a decision. For an in-house team that's already over-capacity, the second is the right tool — it surfaces the gap explicitly rather than burying it in hedges.

**Tuning.** The thresholds in the table above are starting points. After 4 weeks of production traffic, the team should review LOW-classified runs and assess: were they correctly classified, or were they capable of producing a usable deliverable that R11 prevented? Conservative tuning errs on the side of MORE LOW classifications, because the cost of an incomplete-response (re-run with manual research) is far less than the cost of a fabricated deliverable that reaches a client.

**The Confidence sub-agent is NOT the Critic.** The Critic checks for R3/R4/R5 violations and content-level drift. The Confidence sub-agent rolls up everything (including Critic results) into a confidence rating. They run sequentially: Critic → Confidence → R11 gate.

## Interactions with other rules

- **Every other blocking rule** feeds into R11's criteria. R11 is the rollup gate; it doesn't replace the per-rule blocks but ensures that even if all individual rules pass, an aggregate-quality threshold also passes.
- **R13 VerifyFlagCap** directly drives the "verify flags emitted" criterion in R11's table.
- **R15 CompletenessGate** overlaps with R11 — both are roll-up checks. The difference: R15 is about *which sections* of the analysis are complete (definitions, operatives, exemptions, etc.); R11 is about *confidence in each section*. R15 fires first; R11 second.
- **W3 HedgeDensity** is the warning version: hedge frequency above threshold annotates the deliverable but doesn't gate. If hedges + low confidence both fire, R11 wins.

## Test cases

✅ Compliant: all criteria HIGH
```
Run: all blocking rules passed, 0 revise loops, 0 verify flags, all sources fresh, classification overlay complete.
Confidence sub-agent: HIGH on every criterion. Aggregate: HIGH.
Result: deliverable proceeds to Renderer. Slack response delivered with no banner ("DRAFT FOR COUNSEL REVIEW" stamp only).
```

⚠️ Compliant: MEDIUM rating
```
Run: 1 cross-ref unresolved (skipped with reason); 2 revise loops; 1 verify flag.
Confidence: cross-refs MEDIUM, revise-count MEDIUM, verify-flags HIGH. Aggregate: MEDIUM.
Result: deliverable proceeds. Banner added: "⚠️ MEDIUM CONFIDENCE — review the verify flags and the unresolved cross-ref before relying."
```

❌ Non-compliant: LOW rating, missing definitions
```
Run: R9 violation auto-amended at plan time, but the chapter's Definitions section turned out to be unavailable (corpus didn't have it).
Confidence: definitions criterion LOW. Aggregate: LOW.
Result: Renderer not invoked. Incomplete-response emitted:
"RESEARCH INCOMPLETE — DO NOT RELY ON THIS DRAFT
Original question: <verbatim>
Jurisdictions: [US-CA]
Topic: independent contractor classification
What was retrieved: ca-lab-2775; ca-lab-2776
Failed criteria:
- ❌ Definitions retrieved: Chapter 2 (Lab. Code §§ 2750-2773) Definitions section not present in the corpus. Re-ingest CA Labor Code (`agentic-law-os-ingestion ingest-statutes --jurisdiction US-CA --force-refresh`) and re-run.
Next step: re-ingestion recommended.
DRAFT FOR COUNSEL REVIEW (incomplete)"
```

❌ Non-compliant: LOW rating, ≥3 unresolved cross-refs
```
Run: 4 cross-refs unresolved, no skip reasons, after 3 trace iterations.
Confidence: cross-refs LOW. Aggregate: LOW.
Result: incomplete-response listing the 4 cross-refs and recommending manual review or expanding the TaskSpec to include all referenced provisions.
```

❌ Non-compliant: LOW rating, requires_fresh + stale corpus + refresh failed
```
TaskSpec.requires_fresh: true. Span: stale. Refresh attempt: failed (scraper error).
R10 escalates to R11.
Confidence: currency LOW. Aggregate: LOW.
Result: incomplete-response: "Could not confirm currency. Last successful corpus refresh was 2025-12-15; refresh attempt failed at <timestamp> with <error>. Manual refresh required."
```

## Open questions

- **Threshold calibration.** The HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW thresholds in the criteria table are best-guess starting points. After production traffic, review with the team: are LOW classifications too frequent (under-delivering) or too rare (over-delivering)? Calibrate quarterly.
- **Reviewer override.** Should an attorney be able to force-deliver a LOW-rated draft anyway (with a "I accept the risk" toggle)? Current design: no — the agent doesn't permit override. If the attorney wants the partial research, they can request a "research notes" output that explicitly is not a deliverable (Phase 4 enhancement). v1 enforces hard block.
- **Per-criterion weighting.** Currently any single LOW drags aggregate to LOW. An alternative is weighted scoring where less-critical criteria contribute less. Resist this — the criteria were chosen because each is load-bearing. Weighting tends to mask the load-bearing failures.
- **Confidence-vs-cost tradeoff for cheap escalations.** A LOW-confidence answer to a small, low-stakes operational question wastes the requester's time more than delivering with caveats. Worth exploring an `auto_downgrade_to_orientation: true` flag that lets the agent deliver as "orientation only" (not a research memo) when LOW. Phase 4 design.
